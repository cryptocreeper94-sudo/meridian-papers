The Network: Where Meridian Leads

The data internet began as ARPANET — a small experimental network connecting a handful of research computers. The protocol was correct. The addressing was correct. The routing was correct. Everything else followed.

Chapter Four extends the Meridian protocol to internet scale. Chapter Five extends the Meridian mesh to carry both energy and data simultaneously, collapsing two infrastructure problems into one.

Chapter Four
The Energy Internet

The internet did not begin as a global network. It began as a protocol — a formal specification for how independently administered networks could exchange packets across a shared addressing space. Once the protocol was correct, the network grew to encompass the planet without requiring central coordination. The protocol is the internet. The infrastructure follows.

I propose that the same transformation is possible for energy delivery — and that the foundational protocol now exists.

Abstract

I define the Energy Internet Protocol Stack — a formal, layered specification for deterministic energy routing across independently administered meshes: five layers from physical to application. I define the Energy Internet Protocol (EIP) for 128‑bit addressing, ETCP for delivery guarantees, EDNS for human‑readable endpoint naming, and EBGP for inter‑mesh federation.

The supercapacitor is the packet buffer of the Energy Internet. Burst‑mode energy transmission is packet switching applied to power.

4. Energy Addressing (EIP)

EIP Address (128 bits):
  [Global Registry ID : 16 bits]  — assigned by EIRA
  [EAS Number        : 24 bits]   — uniquely identifies mesh operator
  [Zone ID           : 16 bits]   — logical zone within EAS
  [Node Type         : 8 bits]    — source / relay / destination / gateway
  [Node Serial       : 40 bits]   — unique per-node
  [Checksum          : 24 bits]   — integrity verification

EDNS — Energy Domain Name System

[device-name].[zone].[facility].[operator].energy

Examples:

5. Inter‑Mesh Federation (EBGP)

EBGP route advertisements carry safety_certified and guardian_certified flags — a significant departure from data internet BGP, where security properties are not advertised at the routing layer. In the Energy Internet, safety certification is a routing property.

Chapter Bridge: From Energy to Energy‑and‑Data

Every Meridian mesh generates ~11 KB/s of structured, addressed, authenticated data per node — routing table updates, delivery confirmations, security authentication, control‑loop telemetry. This data is currently classified as control‑plane overhead. It is not overhead. It is the first payload of a data communication channel that already exists.

Chapter Five formalizes this: the Unified Energy‑Data Mesh. One installation. Two resource types. No separate communication network required.
